## Rules

1. **Entities**: Use canonical names (e.g., "People's Republic of China" not "China" ‚Äî but include "China" as alias). Merge co-referent mentions.
2. **Entity types ‚Äî strict enforcement**: The `type` field MUST be exactly one of the 13 values listed above. Do NOT invent any other value. Forbidden examples: EVENT, TECHNOLOGY, FACILITY, OPERATION, INCIDENT, CONFLICT, CONCEPT_GROUP. If nothing fits, use CONCEPT as the fallback ‚Äî never coin a new type.
3. **Events are NOT entities**: Operations, battles, attacks, exercises, conflicts, and incidents are NOT entities and must never appear in the `entities` array. Capture what happened, who was involved, and what the outcome was as CLAIMS instead. For example, an airstrike is a claim (factual), not an entity of type EVENT.
4. **Relations**: Only extract a relation when the evidence explicitly supports THAT specific relation, as a current fact (not planned, hoped, or future). Each must reference two entities from your entity list. Prefer the 10 listed relation types below; if none fits well, you may use a descriptive string ‚Äî but prefer the canonical set first. When in doubt, capture it as a CLAIM rather than inventing a relation.
   - **OPERATES**: a country or military unit operates, fields, deploys, uses, or has IN ACTIVE SERVICE a platform or weapon system. The operator must be a COUNTRY or a MILITARY_UNIT (e.g. a navy, air force, or army) and the target a WEAPON_SYSTEM, VESSEL, AIRCRAFT, or SATELLITE. Do NOT use OPERATES with an ORGANIZATION (ministry, agency, or company) as the operator ‚Äî name the country or the specific armed force instead.
   - **OPERATES is current service only, NOT planned/ordered/in-development**: emit OPERATES only for systems CURRENTLY in active service. Planned, ordered, on-order, pledged, announced, "soon", under development, or future joint programs do NOT count ‚Äî capture those as CLAIMS. Do NOT emit `Germany ‚ÄîOPERATES‚Üí Main Ground Combat System` (a future Franco-German program not yet fielded) or `Ukraine ‚ÄîOPERATES‚Üí Gripen` (pledged / "soon", not yet delivered).
   - **OPERATES is NOT manufacturing**: A manufacturer, builder, designer, shipyard, registry, database, or contractor does NOT operate a platform just because it builds, registers, lists, designs, integrates, or supplies it. Do NOT emit `Lockheed Martin ‚ÄîOPERATES‚Üí MK41`, `Raytheon ‚ÄîOPERATES‚Üí AN/SPY-6`, `ThyssenKrupp ‚ÄîOPERATES‚Üí F-127`, or `Naval Vessel Register ‚ÄîOPERATES‚Üí F-127`. If the source says a company builds or supplies a system, emit `SUPPLIES_TO` only when the recipient actor is explicit (e.g., `Lockheed Martin ‚ÄîSUPPLIES_TO‚Üí U.S. Navy`); otherwise capture the builder/supplier fact as a CLAIM, not as an OPERATES relation.
   - **OPERATES is NOT acquiring or attributing**: Do NOT attribute a system to a country/force that does not actually field it (e.g., IRIS-T is German ‚Äî do NOT emit `USA ‚ÄîOPERATES‚Üí IRIS-T`; the E-8 Joint STARS is operated by the U.S. Air Force, not the Space Force).
   - **OPERATES_IN** (strictly locative, evidence-backed): a country or military unit operating IN a geographic location, region, or country, where the evidence actually places it there. Example: `NATO ‚ÄîOPERATES_IN‚Üí Baltic Sea`. Use this ONLY when the target is a LOCATION, REGION, or COUNTRY. Do NOT emit it just because a force belongs to or is based in a country: a unit defined by FOREIGN/overseas operations does NOT "operate in" its home country (e.g. do NOT emit `Quds Force ‚ÄîOPERATES_IN‚Üí Iran` when the evidence describes foreign operations). To say a country operates a weapon, ship, or aircraft, use OPERATES, not OPERATES_IN.
   - **COMMANDS** (explicit operational command only): a person, military unit, or security organization that holds explicit operational command authority over a military unit. Example: `Gerasimov ‚ÄîCOMMANDS‚Üí Russian Armed Forces` (he is the chief of the general staff). RANK, AUTHORSHIP, MEMBERSHIP, or AFFILIATION is NOT command: a colonel, officer, author, theorist, or member of a force does NOT command it. Do NOT emit `Qiao Liang ‚ÄîCOMMANDS‚Üí People's Liberation Army` or `colonel ‚ÄîCOMMANDS‚Üí entire armed forces` ‚Äî capture rank/membership/authorship as a CLAIM. Never target a COUNTRY (`Gerasimov ‚ÄîCOMMANDS‚Üí Russia` is wrong), and do NOT use COMMANDS for civilian/corporate/academic leadership (`Terman ‚ÄîCOMMANDS‚Üí Stanford`, `Perry ‚ÄîCOMMANDS‚Üí ISL Inc.` are wrong).
   - **SUPPLIES_TO** (direction is critical): the SOURCE is the supplier, seller, builder, funder, or contractor; the TARGET is the recipient that receives, buys, or is funded. A customer that BUYS or PROCURES from a vendor is the REVERSE ‚Äî the vendor SUPPLIES_TO the customer. Example: a lab that buys supercomputers from IBM, CDC, or Cray ‚Üí `IBM ‚ÄîSUPPLIES_TO‚Üí Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory`, NEVER `Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory ‚ÄîSUPPLIES_TO‚Üí IBM`. An ACQUISITION (one company buys or acquires another) is NOT SUPPLIES_TO ‚Äî e.g. `Google ‚ÄîSUPPLIES_TO‚Üí Keyhole Inc.` is wrong because Google acquired Keyhole; capture an acquisition as a CLAIM.
   - **TARGETS**: source is the attacker or threatener, target is what is attacked or threatened. Example: `Russia ‚ÄîTARGETS‚Üí Ukraine` (Russia attacks Ukraine, not the reverse).
   - **ALLIED_WITH**: a strategic or military alliance between countries or organizations. A commercial, business, vendor, customer, or technology partnership is NOT an alliance ‚Äî do NOT emit `Hadion ‚ÄîALLIED_WITH‚Üí Palantir Technologies`; capture a business partnership as a CLAIM.
   - **COMPETES_WITH**: only genuine rivals or competitors (countries or organizations). Formal allies are NOT competitors ‚Äî do NOT emit `Germany ‚ÄîCOMPETES_WITH‚Üí USA` or any allies-as-rivals edge. NATO members are allied with each other, not competing.
   - **MEMBER_OF**: only when the evidence documents actual membership or belonging (a person belongs to an organization; an APT/threat group is a unit of an intelligence agency). Capability targets, force commitments, contributions, cooperation, or partnership do NOT establish membership ‚Äî do NOT emit `Luftwaffe ‚ÄîMEMBER_OF‚Üí NATO` merely from NATO capability-goal discussion. Capture a contribution or commitment as a CLAIM.
   - **NEGOTIATES_WITH**: parties in an actual negotiation, talks, or deal-making (diplomatic or commercial). A journalist or newspaper interviewing a person is NOT a negotiation ‚Äî do NOT emit `Der Standard ‚ÄîNEGOTIATES_WITH‚Üí <person>`; capture an interview as a CLAIM.
   - **SANCTIONS**: use as in v4.
5. **Claims**: Distinguish factual (verifiable), assessment (analytical judgment), and prediction (future-oriented). One sentence per claim. No hedging ‚Äî the confidence score captures uncertainty.
6. **Confidence**: 0.9+ = explicitly stated with evidence. 0.7-0.89 = strongly implied. 0.5-0.69 = weakly implied or ambiguous. Below 0.5 = don't extract.
7. **Temporal scope**: Always provide. Use "ongoing" if no time reference is given.
8. **Do NOT invent** information not present in the source. When in doubt, lower confidence rather than omit.
